\section{Materials and Methods}
\label{sec:methods}

\subsection{Scenario construction and simulator credibility}

The public pipeline generated correction scenarios over the frozen F0, F1, and
F2 fidelity levels. The implementation retained mass
depletion, event handling, crew-related constraints, uncertainty variables,
and numerical feasibility checks. The intent was to predict benchmark-defined
cost or feasibility outcomes, not to control a spacecraft. The source and
scenario construction were frozen before the model campaigns; later reports
could summarize the outputs but could not replace source evidence to make a
comparison pass.

Simulator credibility was assessed in Gate 3 by comparing eligible Python
endpoints with GMAT endpoints under the accepted force-model configurations.
The diagnostic chain isolated an Earth-J2 coefficient-file formatting issue
and confirmed the repaired format through standard degree-two, order-zero
comparisons. In the final accepted evidence, all 67 evaluable checks passed;
the repaired endpoint set also passed the frozen position and velocity limits.
This verifies an implementation boundary, not a full mission environment.

One record, RTC3, is explicitly excluded from the claim. Its public qualified
source predates the event under the frozen temporal-evidence rule. It is
therefore classified as \texttt{not\_eligible}, rather than as a successful
or failed endpoint. This distinction prevents an important category error:
source eligibility is not a numerical outcome. The exclusion remains visible
in the evidence package rather than being silently omitted.

\subsection{Frozen development data and leakage control}

Let $\mathcal{D}=\{(x_i,y_i,z_i,g_i)\}_{i=1}^{N}$ denote the frozen
development dataset. Here $x_i$ is the scenario feature vector, $y_i$ is the
robust correction-cost target, $z_i$ is the independently propagated
feasibility label when that endpoint is used, and $g_i$ is a mission-design
group. Groups, rather than individual records, are assigned to development
folds. Thus records sharing a mission-design group do not appear on opposite
sides of a training-validation partition.

All transformations are learned within the training partition of each fold.
This includes imputation, categorical encoding, scaling, target scaling, and
principal-component analysis (PCA) where configured. The requirement is
important because globally fitted scaling or PCA can transmit held-out
structure into a training representation even when the row identifiers are not
directly used. The procedure makes the reported errors more conservative, but
it makes a model comparison interpretable.

The development campaign uses nested hash-selected sample rungs of 128, 256,
512, and 1,024 rows. Selected configurations are evaluated over 20 frozen seed
indices. These are not attempts to estimate an operational distribution; they
are a prespecified stress test for whether a candidate can clear its
development advancement rule across retained group structure and controlled
randomness. Calibration and final-test data were never read by these campaigns.

\subsection{Task definitions and estimands}

The primary endpoint is robust correction-cost prediction. For a fitted
predictor $f$, a fold-level error takes the form
\begin{equation}
\mathrm{NRMSE}(f) =
\frac{1}{s_y}\sqrt{\frac{1}{n}\sum_{i=1}^{n}\left(f(x_i)-y_i\right)^2},
\label{eq:nrmse}
\end{equation}
where $s_y$ is the frozen target scale for the corresponding evaluation
configuration. The primary report uses pooled out-of-fold NRMSE, while
unweighted fold summaries and seed-level diagnostics are retained to expose
heterogeneity. Robust correction cost and feasibility-constrained regret are
interpretation metrics rather than substitutes for the frozen primary endpoint.

The classification track estimates a feasibility score $p_f(x)$. It is evaluated
with Brier score, AUROC, recall, precision, false-negative rate, and
calibration diagnostics. For a fixed threshold $t=0.5$, recall is
\begin{equation}
\mathrm{Recall}_{0.5}=\frac{\mathrm{TP}}{\mathrm{TP}+\mathrm{FN}}.
\label{eq:recall}
\end{equation}
This endpoint has a different scientific role from regression. A feasibility
model with adequate discrimination but poor recall can be unsuitable as a
safety-filter candidate even if its AUROC appears reasonable. The study keeps
these interpretations separate.

The Q01 family is described generically by an encoded-state kernel
\begin{equation}
K_q(x,x')=\left|\langle 0|U_{\phi}(x)^{\dagger}U_{\phi}(x')|0\rangle\right|^2,
\label{eq:qkernel}
\end{equation}
where $U_{\phi}$ denotes the frozen feature-map circuit. Equation
\eqref{eq:qkernel} states the family-level fidelity construction; the exact
feature, PCA, bandwidth, and regularization settings are held in the committed
configuration records. A kernel learner maps the resulting Gram matrix to a
prediction. This representation is not itself a claim of a hardware
implementation or a quantum speedup.

\subsection{Models and matched controls}

The preregistered Q01 candidate is a fidelity-style quantum-kernel regressor
for the correction-cost endpoint. Its principal comparator is C06, a
physics-residual control that combines a low-fidelity physical baseline with a
classical residual learner. C06 is a more demanding comparator than a generic
regressor because it encodes useful problem structure available to both the
scenario and the benchmark. The registered comparison set also contains A01
random Fourier features, compressed classical views, and other frozen
classical-family configurations. The low-fidelity reference and explicit
cost--feasibility separation follow trajectory-surrogate precedents, while A01
tests whether a classical random-feature representation can explain an
apparent quantum feature effect \cite{steffen2025,xie2022,rahimi2007}.

After the accepted Gate 5 result, P001 defined two prospective exploratory
tracks without reopening the primary campaign. Q01b uses reduced-density
matrix projections and a median-distance projected kernel. FQK applies a
quantum kernel only to the independently propagated feasibility label. Both
tracks retained groupwise splits, fold-local preprocessing, nested rungs,
matched controls, and 20 seed indices. They were allowed to produce a new
development-only signal, but not to revise the decision already made for Q01.

The D034--D049 successor branch tightened this discipline further. Each
protocol froze its candidate, target, comparator, threshold, stopping rule,
and source path before fitting. Several candidates produced a lower NRMSE than
C06. That alone was insufficient. A candidate had to survive its matched
classical alternative and branch-specific rule, so a gain attributable to a
classical residual construction could not be labeled quantum-specific.

\subsection{Human-centered decision-support boundary}

The reported model outputs are treated as evidence for a future screening tool,
not as a command to change a trajectory. A human reviewer would need the model
estimate, the applicable scenario group, its comparator result, its uncertainty
record, and an explicit statement of whether the case is inside the authorized
scope. The research package preserves those items as source records and figure
provenance rather than reducing them to a single leaderboard value.

This stance also defines the fallback: when a candidate is ineligible, poorly
calibrated, source-ineligible, or outside the public benchmark boundary, the
model is not promoted and the physical or numerical reference remains the
decision basis. This is a design boundary for later human--AI interaction; it
does not evaluate an interface or the behavior of mission personnel.

\subsection{Statistical decision process}

The preregistered Gate 5 trigger required complete eligible folds and seeds,
comparison with the strongest classical finalist and matched dequantization
controls, paired uncertainty checks, and a qualifying regime defined before
outcomes were observed. The reporting package retains paired bootstrap
intervals, fold-level sign diagnostics, and a sign-permutation test with
multiplicity control. It does not add a post-outcome seed, model, split, or
threshold to repair a weak result. Learning curves and retained seed outcomes
are reported because training fit or a single favorable run is not adequate
evidence of generalization \cite{peters2023,grant2019}.

The distinction between task completion and eligibility is also retained. D006
completed 871 of 871 authorized tasks with zero task failures. Q02 and Q03
were not carried to later rungs because they did not meet the frozen retention
rule. Their status is therefore
\texttt{not\_reached\_under\_frozen\_eligibility}. It does not mean a
failed experiment, a missing computation, or a favorable result withheld from
the report.

\subsection{Computational-resource and reproducibility boundary}

The reference compute environment was a Windows laptop with a 13th Generation
Intel Core i9-13900HX CPU, 32~GB RAM, and an NVIDIA GeForce RTX 4060 Laptop
GPU. The experiment applied a bounded-RAM and serialized-statevector policy;
the GPU and quantum hardware were not used for the reported QML evidence. The
hardware description is included so that readers understand the throughput and
resource constraints under which the public benchmark was executed. It is not
a prescription that future studies use identical hardware.

The source release contains configuration records, seeds, data cards, figure
registry entries, machine-readable tables, and reporting scripts. Every figure
used in this paper is copied with its corresponding registry entry into the
paper package. These measures make the evidence auditable while preserving the
lock on calibration and final-test data.
