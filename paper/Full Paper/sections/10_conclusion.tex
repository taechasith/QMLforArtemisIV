\section{Conclusion}

Under the frozen public-data development benchmark, the tested QML candidates
did not outperform strong classical controls, and no candidate from the Gate 5
or D034--D049 branches is eligible for a Gate 6 mission experiment. The
primary Q01 quantum kernel, the projected Q01b kernel, and the feasibility-only
FQK track all produced negative outcomes under their stated criteria. The
strongest successor improvement over C06 did not persist above its matched
classical-control threshold.

The study's contribution is a reproducible negative result with an explicit
governance and claim structure. It reports what was tested, what did not
advance, what is merely a future lesson, and what is outside the evidence. It
does not establish quantum advantage, fuel savings, mission utility, hardware
performance, flight readiness, NASA approval, or an invented QML model. That
restraint is necessary for the result to be useful.
