# D039: Error-Conditioned Global Fidelity Residual Kernel

Protocol: P007
Status: accepted for one bounded development-only campaign
Date: 2026-07-15

## 1. Research problem

Can the first sub-threshold GFRK signal be strengthened by making the
task-aligned representation explicitly conditional on the honest C06 baseline
prediction, without changing the global fidelity kernel or the evaluation
contract?

## 2. Mathematical formulation

For each outer fold `f`, let `c_i` be the fold-local compressed feature and let
`b_f(x)` be the outer C06 cost prediction. The training projection receives
`u_i = [c_i, b_f^{-inner}(x_i)]`, where the C06 feature is generated by inner
cross-fitting. The outer validation projection receives
`u_i = [c_i, b_f(x_i)]`; no validation outcome is used. Let `e_i` be the
cross-fitted C06 residual and let `z_i = P_q(u_i; e)` be the standardized
fold-local PLS score. The state is

`|psi(z)> = U_RY/RZ,CNOT(z)|0^q>`.

The quantum correction is `delta_Q(x) = K_F(z,Z) alpha`, where
`K_F(z,v) = |<psi(z)|psi(v)>|^2`, and the prediction is

`f_Q(x) = b_f(x) + 0.10 * delta_Q(x)`.

The matched classical control uses the same `z`, landmarks, ridge solve, and
lambda with an exact RBF kernel. The C06 feasibility head is unchanged.

## 3. Definitions and assumptions

`q` is 4, 6, or 8; one entangling layer is fixed; 256 deterministic landmarks
are used; and lambda is fixed at 0.10 before fitting. The C06 feature is
standardized by the outer-fold target standardizer and is generated without
validation targets. Only development rows are allowed.

## 4. Scientific status of claims

Cross-fitted baseline conditioning is a design hypothesis motivated by residual
learning. The PLS and prediction equations are new protocol definitions. The
fidelity kernel PSD property is established for normalized state embeddings.
Improvement is an empirical hypothesis. No NASA-performance, mission,
hardware, quantum-advantage, Gate 5, or Gate 6 claim is authorized.

## 5. Closest prior method

The closest method is D038 GFRK. D039 adds one declared baseline-prediction
feature before the same PLS, global fidelity kernel, shrinkage, and classical
control. D038 is immutable and is not rerun.

## 6. Reproduction plan

Reproduce the D038 five-fold C06 cross-fitting and use the resulting training
predictions as the additional PLS input feature. Generate the outer validation
C06 prediction from training rows only, fit the error-conditioned projection on
training rows, and apply global fidelity and identical-input RBF corrections.

## 7. Reproduction success criteria

All five outer folds, four inner folds, q=4/6/8, and 20 seeds must complete.
The error-conditioned feature must have the expected training and validation
shape, projection audits must report zero validation-outcome use, groups must
be disjoint, statevectors must be normalized, and locked-data counters must
remain zero.

## 8. Proposed modification

Append the cross-fitted C06 prediction to the compressed feature before the
fold-local residual-supervised PLS fit. No kernel, shrinkage, split, optimizer,
threshold, seed, safety head, or data boundary changes.

## 9. Proposed mechanism

The residual target is defined relative to C06. Conditioning the representation
on the baseline prediction may let PLS separate correction geometry by the
baseline's predicted error level. The identical-input RBF tests whether any
gain is due only to the new classical coordinate map.

## 10. Main hypothesis

EC-GFRK-08-L010 will reduce pooled OOF NRMSE by at least 5 percent versus C06,
preserve selection safety, and beat EC-TAP-RBF-SHR-q8-L010 by at least 5 percent.

## 11. Falsification criteria

Reject if any primary improvement, paired interval, safety metric, classical
control comparison, feature provenance check, state normalization, or fold/seed
audit fails.

## 12. Candidate theorem or proposition

If all input rows are finite and the C06 prediction feature is generated by a
training-only model, appending it to the compressed feature does not create
access to outer validation outcomes. The fidelity kernel remains positive
semidefinite for normalized state embeddings.

## 13. Proof strategy

The data-access statement follows from the construction: inner predictions use
only inner fit groups and outer validation predictions use only outer training
rows. For the kernel, map each input to
`rho_z=|psi(z)><psi(z)|`; then
`Tr(rho_z rho_v)=|<psi(z)|psi(v)>|^2`, whose Gram quadratic form is
nonnegative. These arguments prove provenance and PSD, not performance.

## 14. Counterexample search

Test identical states, orthogonal states, one-qubit states, repeated baseline
features, constant baseline features, no entanglement, normalized-state
perturbations, zero residual targets, and a validation feature perturbed after
fit. Verify finite shapes, unchanged training rows, symmetry, and PSD after the
prescribed numerical clipping.

## 15. Experimental design

Use five outer grouped folds, 1,024 training rows per fold, four inner grouped
folds, q=4/6/8, one entangling layer, lambda=0.10, 20 seeds, all outer
validation rows, and the unchanged C06 safety head. The primary is q=8 with
lambda=0.10.

## 16. Classical baselines

C06 is the primary baseline. EC-TAP-RBF-SHR-q uses the identical
error-conditioned PLS scores, landmarks, gamma, ridge solve, residual targets,
and lambda as EC-GFRK.

## 17. Quantum baselines

D034 PRQK, D035 CFQSR, D036 TAP-QK, D037 TSQR, and D038 GFRK remain immutable
historical references. The tested statevector kernel is classically simulable
at q<=8, so no quantum advantage claim is possible.

## 18. Ablation studies

q=4/6/8 is the fixed width ablation. The D038 no-baseline-feature result is a
historical comparison, not a post-outcome reranking. Removing the baseline
feature inside D039 would be an unregistered ablation and is not authorized.

## 19. Statistical analysis

Report all 20 seed-pooled OOF NRMSE, MAE, regret, infeasible selection, Brier,
AUROC, and recall values, with means, sample standard deviations, and paired
95% bootstrap intervals for EC-GFRK-08-L010 minus C06. Apply the same fixed
decision rule to the matched classical control.

## 20. Resource analysis

Record q, circuit depth, two-qubit gates, statevector dimension, landmarks,
kernel evaluations, PLS input width, PSD clipping, wall time, CPU time, peak
working set, free disk, state preparation, and classical decoding.

## 21. Noise analysis

Finite-shot and hardware-noise tests remain deferred unless the exact
statevector primary passes. Noise cannot rescue a negative exact endpoint.

## 22. Scaling analysis

Report q=4/6/8 and the added one-feature PLS input width. Do not infer
asymptotic quantum scaling from three bounded sizes.

## 23. Classical-simulability analysis

Exact q<=8 statevectors and fidelity matrices are efficiently classically
simulable. A positive result would be a hybrid-surrogate result, not quantum
advantage.

## 24. Data-access cost

All data are classical development rows. Include inner C06 fitting, outer C06
fitting, feature append, PLS fitting, state preparation, state overlap,
Nyström solve, shrinkage, and decoding. No QRAM or quantum-memory assumption
is made.

## 25. Reproducibility requirements

Commit the protocol, config, launcher, feature/projection/inner/fidelity
audits, seed metrics, summaries, paired interval, figures, registry rows,
source commit, and zero locked-data counters.

## 26. Main scientific risks

The added baseline feature may merely make the classical RBF stronger, may
cause projection collinearity, or may overfit the residual target. A small
gain may remain below the preregistered threshold.

## 27. Main implementation risks

The risks are using the low-fidelity baseline instead of cross-fitted C06,
using validation outcomes to create the feature, inconsistent feature order,
and claiming that a lower NRMSE is quantum-specific. The launcher fails closed
on these conditions.

## 28. Minimum publishable result

A complete valid negative identifies whether error-conditioned coordinates add
anything beyond D038 and records the result as future invention evidence. A
positive requires every C06, classical-control, safety, integrity, and resource
condition before any operational interpretation.

## 29. Recommended first experiment

Run all declared q values with q=8/lambda=0.10 as the single primary endpoint
and EC-TAP-RBF-SHR as the matched control.

## 30. Final assessment

EC-GFRK is mathematically explicit, directly motivated by the D038 signal, and
bounded for the reference laptop. Its result is unresolved until the
source-bound campaign executes.

Current conclusion: **Proceed only after reproduction**.
